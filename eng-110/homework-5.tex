\begin{homeworkProblem}
    \textbf{Read Brummel “Practical Experience Trumps Fancy Degrees” and write a summary of article.}

    In ``Practical Experience Trumps Fancy Degrees'', Tony Brummel juxstaposes 
    practical, ``real life'', knowledge and the theoretical content of a 
    bachelor's degree. Brummel believes that one's success in business is 
    independent of any form of degree. To support brings forth the fact that 
    most Business professors have never \emph{actually} started a business. 
    Brummel argues from experience, himself having trailed a successful path 
    independent of college education; he started a record label that now 
    grosses at over \$20 million, having released over 500 albums. Brummel 
    points out that, since he did not go to college, he had to learn the 
    foundations of business on his own, through trial-and-error, and that 
    knowledge has stuck with him throughout 20 years of business. Finally, 
    Brummel advocates that if you have good employees, advisers, legal backup, 
    and a brand that people care about you will succeed.    
\end{homeworkProblem}
